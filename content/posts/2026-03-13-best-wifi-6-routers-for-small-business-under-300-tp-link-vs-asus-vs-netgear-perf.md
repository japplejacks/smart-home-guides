## Why WiFi 6 Matters for Small Business

Before we jump into specific router comparisons, let's understand why upgrading to WiFi 6 is crucial for modern small businesses. WiFi 6 (802.11ax) delivers up to 40% higher speeds than WiFi 5, but speed isn't the only advantage.

The real game-changer is efficiency. WiFi 6 uses technologies like OFDMA (Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access) and Target Wake Time to handle multiple devices simultaneously without the performance degradation you'd see with older standards. For a small business with 10-30 connected devices, this translates to smoother video calls, faster file transfers, and less network congestion.

## Setup and Configuration for Small Business

### Initial Setup Process

All three routers offer both web-based and mobile app setup options. From a business perspective, here's what to expect:

1. **Physical Installation**: Place the router centrally, away from interference sources like microwaves or metal filing cabinets
2. **Network Configuration**: Set up separate networks for employees and guests
3. **Security Setup**: Enable WPA3 encryption and change default admin passwords
4. **QoS Configuration**: Prioritize business-critical applications like VoIP and video conferencing
5. **Guest Network**: Configure with appropriate bandwidth limits

### Pro Tips for Business Configuration

- Use separate SSIDs for 2.4GHz and 5GHz bands to maintain control over device connections
- Set up scheduled reboots during off-hours to maintain optimal performance
- Enable automatic firmware updates for security patches
- Document your configuration for future reference or troubleshooting
